Chapati

Chapati, a staple in South Asian cuisine, has its origins in the Indian subcontinent. It is a type of unleavened flatbread made from whole wheat flour, water, and salt. The dough is rolled into thin circles and cooked on a hot griddle or skillet until it puffs up and develops brown spots. Chapatis are typically cooked without oil, although some variations may use a small amount of oil or ghee. The simplicity of its ingredients and cooking method makes chapati a versatile and popular accompaniment to a wide range of dishes, from curries to vegetables, and it is enjoyed by people across the region for its taste and texture.

Pizza

Pizza originated in Naples, Italy, in the 18th century and was initially a simple dish made with flatbread, tomato, garlic, and olive oil. It gained popularity among the working class for its affordability and delicious flavor. Today, pizza has evolved into a diverse dish with countless variations worldwide. The traditional Neapolitan pizza, known for its thin crust and minimal toppings, is still revered for its simplicity and quality ingredients. Modern pizza can be topped with a variety of ingredients, including cheese, meats, vegetables, and herbs, and is typically baked in a wood-fired oven to achieve a crispy crust and flavorful toppings.

Croissant

The croissant, a buttery and flaky pastry, is believed to have originated in Austria during the 17th century. It was inspired by the kipferl, a crescent-shaped baked good enjoyed in Austria. The croissant as we know it today became popular in France in the 19th century. The French adapted the pastry, making it lighter and flakier by incorporating more butter into the dough and perfecting the technique of rolling and folding the dough to create layers. To prepare croissants, dough made from flour, yeast, milk, sugar, salt, and butter is rolled out, folded multiple times, and then shaped into crescent shapes. The pastries are then baked until golden brown and served warm, often with a filling such as chocolate or almond paste.

Fried Fish

Fried fish has been a culinary tradition in many cultures for centuries. Its exact origin is difficult to pinpoint, as frying food is a cooking technique that has been used worldwide for a long time. However, it is believed that the practice of battering and frying fish may have originated in Western Europe, particularly in countries with access to abundant seafood. In this method, fish fillets or whole fish are coated in a seasoned batter or breading and then deep-fried until crispy and golden brown. This cooking technique not only adds flavor and texture but also helps preserve the fish. Fried fish is enjoyed in various cuisines globally, often served with chips or other accompaniments.
